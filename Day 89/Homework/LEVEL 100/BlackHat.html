Typical (high-level) activities — no instructions

Malware deployment: spreading ransomware, banking trojans, or remote access tools (RATs). Phishing & social engineering: tricking people into revealing credentials or installing malware. Credential stuffing / account takeover: using leaked usernames/passwords to access services. Exploitation of vulnerabilities: taking advantage of unpatched software to gain access. Data exfiltration: copying and removing sensitive files. Command-and-control: maintaining covert access to compromised systems. DDoS attacks: overwhelming services to make them unavailable.

Black hat hackers!

Black hat hackers are individuals or groups who break into systems, steal data, disrupt services, or cause damage for malicious reasons: financial gain, espionage, sabotage, ideology, or reputation. They operate illegally and without permission.

Common motives


Financial profit: ransomware, theft of payment data, bank fraud. Data theft / resale: selling personal data, credentials, intellectual property. Espionage: corporate or state-sponsored theft of secrets. Disruption / sabotage: denial-of-service attacks, disabling infrastructure. Notoriety / challenge: reputation building in criminal communities or “bragging rights.” Political/ideological goals: hacktivism to promote causes.
