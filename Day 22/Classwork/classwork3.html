Donatello (c. 1386–1466)was an Italian sculptor and a key figure of the Early Renaissance. He brought lifelike emotion and realism to sculpture, breaking from the stiff medieval style. He's best known for his bronze David—the first free-standing nude statue since ancient times—and his powerful Gattamelata equestrian statue. Donatello's work set the stage for later masters like Michelangelo.
